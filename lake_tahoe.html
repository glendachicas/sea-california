Napa Valley is best known for it's wine and gorgeous wineries. Some of the best wine in the world is made here!


History


Lake Tahoe is about two million years old. Native Americans found this lake and called it home. The Washoe Tribe used this lad to camp, hunt, and fish. The Washoe people considered the lake sacred because it contains life-sustaining water. The lake's name 'Tahoe' was due to a pronunciation of the Washoe word - Da ow a ga which means 'edge of the lake'. The 1850s was the height of the California Gold Rush and brought visitors and prospectors. Many people and events have contributed to the Tahoe that we know today. Tahoe aims to protect and preserve the natural beauty and resources of this beautiful place. Today, about 15 million people visit Lake Tahoe per year. Recommended Activities


Tahoe Tree Top Adventure Park!


Tahoe Tree Top Adventure Park provides engaging active outdoor activities by pushing oneself by learning and discovery. The park offers experiences geared towards solo adventures or group adventures. The park encourages self growth through breaking self perceived limits to create and strengthen bonds between self, family, friends,etc. The park offers a variety of courses and many bridges. This is an interactive experience that last around 2+ hours.

When in Lake Tahoe, Tahoe Tree Top Adventure Park is a must visit. Keep in mind that there is a fee to enter the park.Tickets start at $59. If you want learn more about Tahoe Tree Top Adventures or for tickets, click here: Tahoe Tree Top


Donner Memorial State Park


Donner Memorial State Park is located in the Sierra Nevada mountain range and has an elevation of about 6,000 feet. In the summer, the park offers camping, picnicking, boating, fishing, and hiking. In the winter, the park offers cross-country skiing, snowshoeing on trails and playing in the snow. There is no fee to enter the park, but a parking pass is required. The park is open year round so there is something to do in every season.

Parking passes average $10 per vehicle.

Hike Robert Louis Stevenson State Park


Robert Louis Stevenson State Park is famously known as the place where the author of Treasure Island and Kidnapped spent his honeymoon. There are no remains of the cabin where the author stayed, but the site is seen on one of the trails. The hike features tough terrain that includes evergreen forests in the canyons on north-facing slopes. The hike to the top of Mt. St. Helena is about five miles, once at the top, one can see the San Francisco Bay Area. The best reason to visit this park is in the spring or fall.

There is no fee at Robert Louis Stevenson State Park. There is limited parking so get there early! Check out the park's website and access maps click, here

Where to eat


Hungry? Check out some of the best restaurants in Napa Valley

  • Oxbow Public Market

    • Oxbow Public market is located in Oxbow District of Napa. This market has become a gathering place for excellent food and wine in downtown Napa. The market features a scenic outdoor seating area and offers a diverse mixture of local food vendors, cafes and organic produce from local farmers. The market supports the idea of sustainable agriculture and harvest. The idea is supposed to promote a healthy environment, social and economic equity within the community. The goal of the market is to create an environment within Oxbow Public Market to improve local food education, community outreach and suppport small businesses and farms. For hours of operations and locations, click here

  • The French Laundry

    • The French Laundry commits itself in serving classic French cuisine with the finest ingredients along with impeccable guest service. The restaurant was establised in the 1990s by Chef Thomas Keller whose dream was to open a fine French cuisine establishment. In 2007, the restaurant was awarded its first Michelin Star. It has received the "Outstanding Restaurant Award" three times since 2007. When at The French Laundry, try the Charcoal Grilled Japanese Wagyu. For more information about The French Laundry, click here.

  • The CIA at Copia

    • The CIA at Copia is presented by The Culinary Institue of America with the intention of gathering to "Feed Your Joy". The CIA at Copia offers its guest the opportunity to take cooking or beverage classes in their kichen. Guest can enjoy seasonal dishes prepared by CIA chefs in their outdoor dining space and have the opportunity to partake in a 3D animation tasting menu. There is something for everyone at CIA at Copia. All procedes from the experiences offered are used for future chefs. For more information about CIA at Copia, click here.

  • Bistro Don Giovanni

    • Bistro Don Giovanni finds inspiration from local farms and the restaurant's garden to create its daily menu. The restaurant features traditional Italian dishes using sustainable ingredients from local farmers and vendors. The restaurant bakes focaccia and ciabatta bread daily and handcrafts its pastas. Some guest favorites include - Beet and Haricort Verts salad, Mama Concetta's meatballs and seared salmon filet. For more information about Bistro Don Giovanni, click here.
